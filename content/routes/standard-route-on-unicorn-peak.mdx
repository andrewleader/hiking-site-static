A reasonably short (5 mile round trip, 2,561 ft gain) ski+tiny rock climb objective! With nearly 2,000 ft of open slope downhill skiing! You do have to put your skins back on to travel the remaining 1 mile back to the trailhead

![](https://andrewbares.blob.core.windows.net/hiking-blog-images/2023/05/image.FdkAmHOx-1024x470.png)

## Climb

[Credit to [SummitPost](https://www.summitpost.org/unicorn-peak/151196#:~:text=Unicorn%20Peak%20is%20the%20tallest,5th%20class%20technical%20rock%20climbing.)] Described below are a few options one has to climb the summit block. They are all fun climbs and each offers a unique challenge. Choose your route based on the comfort level of your group and by the gear you brought on the climb. Each route is 40-50 feet long so a 30 meter rope is enough to both climb any of these routes and make the 50 foot rappel back down. A small rack of&nbsp;**cams from 0.3"-1"**&nbsp;and a set of stoppers will be plenty for each of these routes. Once everyone in your group reaches the gnarled tree belay, it's a 10 second walk to the summit. Don't forget to peer down the north face while you're up there and admire the fantastic view of Mount Rainier.[](https://www.summitpost.org/view_object.php?object_id=21109)Routes on the Unicorn Summit Pinnacle ('XX' are anchors)
