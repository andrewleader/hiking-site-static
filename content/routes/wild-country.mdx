## Overview

- Approach: 1 hr
- Climb: ~3 hrs
- Descent: 2 hours (walk off, bring shoes with you)
- Sounds very well bolted

## Gear

- 15 quickdraws (or 18-26 quickdraws if linking, including 6 alpine)
- Carry shoes for walk off

## Linking options

- P1-2
- P3-4
- P5-6
- P7
- P8-9

## Approach

## P1: 5.7, 30m, ~7 bolts

Easy slab (skip bolts to link) Double bolt anchor.

Can link with P2 (~15 bolts)

- 

## P2: 5.7, 30m, ~8 bolts

Easy slab to nice belay ledge Double bolt anchor.

## P3: 5.10a, 30m, ~14 bolts

Move up and right on some balancy moves on a little bit of sharp rock. Double bolt anchor.

Can link with P4 (~26 bolts) (but can have rough rope drag)

## P4: 5.9, 30m, ~12 bolts

Move up and left on easy slab. Double bolt anchor. (From the starting ledge of P4, there's a separate 5.11 route a bit to the right, don't follow that).

## P5: 5.9, 30m, ~12 bolts

Varied climbing up to a double bolt anchor.

Can link with P6 (~23 bolts)

- P5

## P6: 5.10b, 30m, ~11 bolts

Move right from the anchor to some reach moves to the right and a few tough pulls on thin sharp rock. Double bolt anchor.

- P6 crux (3 bolts up)

## P7: 5.10a, 30m, ~12 bolts

Head up on more grey rock. Double bolt anchor.

Do NOT link

## P8: 5.8, 30m, ~15 bolts

Easy climbing leads to the base of a sort of dihedral formation. Double bolt anchor.

Can link with P9 (~25 bolts)

## P9: 5.9, 30m, ~10 bolts

Climb the awesome slightly overhung rock with amazing exposure on big holds to the top. Double bolt anchor.

- P9
- Topping out
