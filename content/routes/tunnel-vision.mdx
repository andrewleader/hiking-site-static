## Time estimates

About 5 hours C2C?

- 30 min approach
- 3 hrs climbing
- 1 hr descent

## Approach

About 30 minutes. 1.1 miles, 350 ft gain.

## Gear

- #0.3-4 with double 0.4-2. Most say you don't need a #5.
- Nuts (most seem to say a couple nuts were useful)
- Carry approach shoes up (for walk off)

## Overview

![](https://andrewbares.blob.core.windows.net/hiking-blog-images/2025/04/5300639_medium_15578540241.YSqXJoVO.jpg)

## P1 - 5.6

Climb the right facing corner for 15', then hand traverse at the roof right for a few feet until the main crack system is reached. Follow this crack system up, then through a chimney to the **first belay (gear belay)**. Make sure to watch out for rope drag around the opening corner.

![P1](https://andrewbares.blob.core.windows.net/hiking-blog-images/2025/04/image.Fbn91MG8.png)
![](https://andrewbares.blob.core.windows.net/hiking-blog-images/2025/04/110216555_large_14943684381.Nl88zuah-1024x768.jpg)

## P2 - 5.4

Chimney up the wide easy chimney, **belaying at a bolted anchor**.

![](https://andrewbares.blob.core.windows.net/hiking-blog-images/2025/04/image.pLfVMoYS-1024x898.png)

## P3 - 5.7

Another chimney pitch. This pitch is hard to protect, and is steeper and more narrow than the previous chimney. **Belay at a huge ledge (gear anchor)**.

![](https://andrewbares.blob.core.windows.net/hiking-blog-images/2025/04/image.qMqSblUU-914x1024.png)

## P4 - 5.5, 40m

Continue up to **belay at another huge ledge (gear anchor)**.

![](https://andrewbares.blob.core.windows.net/hiking-blog-images/2025/04/image.FeJLxWWU-1024x855.png)

## P5 - 5.3

Climb left into the vertical tunnel. This is a very interesting and unique pitch. However, the rock is quite slick, and pro is scarce until climbing out the other side of the tunnel. Finish with a short crack to **belay at another big ledge**.

![](https://andrewbares.blob.core.windows.net/hiking-blog-images/2025/04/image.wUrSVM-1024x874.png)

![P5](https://andrewbares.blob.core.windows.net/hiking-blog-images/2025/04/112868971_large_14943193941.McIRtNp-768x1024.jpg)

## P6 - 5.5

Climb the excellent handcrack in the left facing dihedral to the top of the climb.

![P6](https://andrewbares.blob.core.windows.net/hiking-blog-images/2025/04/119996758_large_1609980177_topo1.LFfJhMt2-768x1024.jpg)

## Descent

The descent is an easy gully system that has an optional one or two rope rappel en route. This is to the left of the climb.

![](https://andrewbares.blob.core.windows.net/hiking-blog-images/2025/04/image.3oE0Btc6-1024x861.png)
