Overall, this climb was fun but the approach and rappels were annoying. It's something I'd recommend doing once, but I'm not sure I'd do it again considering Mile High Club is a bit better! Really awesome that someone bolted this route though, I'm thankful for those folks!

Key notes...

- Walkie talkies could have been useful to maintain full communication on the last three pitches if desired. The last pitch loses communication completely between anchors. The previous two pitches temporarily lose communication with your follower but otherwise can still communicate from the anchors themselves. I'd probably still skip the walkie talkies though.
- Linking the first two pitches could be possible, but the others would probably be a pain.
- Rappelling is a bit annoying. The anchors always seem to have a lot of friction making pulling the rope a bit difficult.
- Belay ledges are all pretty good! Almost all of them were pretty comfy for two people. Three or four people might be crowded.

## Approach

The normal Sunrise Mine Trail is pretty fine, but when the approach splits from the normal Mile High Club, you have to ascend through a lot of steep loose scree/boulders... it was quite annoying. Slowed things down more than expected. The scree/boulders are always on the verge of sliding down a foot or two. Definitely would not want to be here with a group ahead on the ascent sending down rocks!

## Pitch 1 - 5.9, 100', 4 bolts

For the first pitch, we started belaying before the terrain got too steep. The first bolt is a ways up though but mostly on class 4 terrain till then. For the middle section, I stayed a bit in the dirty corner. The final bolt is the crux, the route turns blank and slabby there! I climbed far right and then did a reachy traverse across the blank face, but Alexis climbed left of the bolt more directly to the anchor, and that seemed to work well.

## Pitch 4 - 5.9, 115', 9-10 bolts

When in doubt go left on ridge, first half is scramble then one crux if you go on overhanging start of ridge on left, can bypass on right, rest is easy. Two more alpines would have been nice. Communicating with the follower when they go behind the ridge can be difficult but otherwise comms between anchors is fine!

## Pitch 5 - 5.10-, 110', 11 bolts

Again, when in doubt traverse left. At the end, if you see a pair of shiny rap chains, DON'T go to that anchor... sharp traverse left and you'll find a hidden bolt behind a rock and then traverse to the bolted anchor (no chains) on the left. The other anchor is for rappelling. One kinda fun crux move otherwise easy, some juggy holds with small feet in a spot too. Comms once again can be difficult when follower is mid-route.

## Pitch 7 - 5.10-, 100', 9 bolts

The start slightly traverses left but nothing bad, and then goes over another bulge. This one is also quite cruxy, with subpar hands this time, but you can work your feet up and trust the subpar hands, and it actually worked! After the bulge, terrain eases and stays left around a block before the summit and then cuts up right to the summit. You lose communication with your belayer.
