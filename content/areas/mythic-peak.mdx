## Approach

From Elbow Lake Trailhead, you soon have to cross the Middle Fork. It's quite a big river. Hopefully there's still a tree across as seen in the first photo. The log was about 1/5th of a mile upstream from Elbow Lake parking.

- ![](https://andrewbares.blob.core.windows.net/hiking-blog-images/2020/05/MiddleFork1.6NNShgqs-1024x683.jpg)Crossing Middle Fork, Aug 5th 2017
- ![](https://andrewbares.blob.core.windows.net/hiking-blog-images/2020/05/MiddleFork2.G2GPJBX-1024x683.jpg)Old bridge on Middle Fork

Then, follow blue diamonds that mark the climbers path into Green Creek Valley!

- ![](https://andrewbares.blob.core.windows.net/hiking-blog-images/2020/05/Diamonds.tpY87eqm-1024x683.jpg)Blue markers on "trail" into Green Creek Basin

Depart the Elbow Lake trail for some bushwacking following up to Green Creek.
